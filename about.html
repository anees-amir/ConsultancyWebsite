About me

I help organisations build AI systems that don't require a choice between insight and privacy

I came to federated learning through my doctoral research, where I spent four years studying how machine learning models behave when data cannot be centralised. What started as a theoretical question — can you train a good model without ever seeing the raw data? — became a practical obsession. The answer, it turns out, is yes. But only if the system is designed carefully from the ground up.

After completing my PhD I worked with several research groups and industry partners applying FL in real environments — healthcare imaging, financial services, and large-scale scientific computing. Each project taught me that the hardest part of federated learning is rarely the algorithm. It is the gap between what is theoretically possible and what is practically deployable in an organisation with real data, real infrastructure, and real regulatory constraints.

AI Privacy Consultancy exists to close that gap. I work with a small number of clients at a time, which means every engagement gets my full attention — from the initial feasibility assessment through to a running system. I do not subcontract the technical work, and I do not sell solutions before I understand the problem.

Why federated learning

A personal note

I think a lot of AI development has proceeded on an assumption that data sharing is either fine or someone else's problem. It is neither. The organisations I work with — hospitals, banks, research institutes — hold data that genuinely cannot and should not leave their walls. For a long time that meant they simply could not benefit from collaborative machine learning. Federated learning changes that.

What I find compelling about this field is that it forces you to take privacy seriously at the design stage, not as a compliance checkbox at the end. A federated system that leaks data through gradient inversion is not a private system — it just looks like one. Doing this properly requires understanding the threat model, the regulatory environment, and the mathematics of the privacy guarantees you are actually providing.

That is the work I find most interesting, and it is why this consultancy exists as a specialist practice rather than a general AI agency. If you are trying to solve a problem that requires keeping data where it lives, I would enjoy talking with you about it.
